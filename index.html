01 / The problem

The same evidence.
Collected twice.

Small fleets rebuild their operation for a broker at renewal, then rebuild it again for an insurer after a loss. The handoff is slow, fragmented, and expensive.

At renewal

Reconstruct the risk

  • Vehicle schedules and ownership
  • Driver and trip activity
  • Maintenance and loss history
  • Coverage states and exclusions
After a loss

Reconstruct the claim

  • Photos, estimates, and invoices
  • Trip and responsibility evidence
  • Repair status and vehicle downtime
  • Emails, portals, and follow-ups

02 / The workflow

A brokerage that remembers.

Indem turns a fleet's operating records into structured insurance work, while licensed professionals retain control of advice, placement, binding, and regulated claims activity.

  1. 01

    Connect the operation

    Bring together vehicles, drivers, trips, maintenance, policies, repairs, and historical claims.

  2. 02

    Build the risk file

    Maintain a source-linked record for each vehicle instead of rebuilding a static application every year.

  3. 03

    Run the brokerage work

    Prepare market-specific submissions, track requirements, compare terms, and service the account.

  4. 04

    Carry evidence into the claim

    Assemble the timeline and supporting records, then coordinate recovery through qualified partners.

03 / Quote to recovery

The policy should know how the fleet actually runs.

Rental vehicles move between on-rent, off-rent, maintenance, and repair states. Indem preserves those changes so the risk is easier to explain and the next claim does not begin from a blank folder.

Structured intake from existing records
Persistent carrier and repair follow-up
Licensed review for regulated actions

Built around the operator, not a form.
